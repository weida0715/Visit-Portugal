Tap Water Quality

The quality of tap water is excellent in Portugal. According to figures from official surveys from Ersar (Portuguese water network) , the percentage of drinking water in mainland Portugal is 98.66%, making it a benchmark at European level.

Common Local Diseases

Portugal has a population of 10.5 million as of 2016, and a mortality rate of 548.6 deaths per 100,000 people. The top ten most common diseases in Portugal in 2016 were ischemic heart disease, cerebrovascular disease, Alzheimer's disease, lower respiratory infections, COPD, colorectal cancer, lung cancer, diabetes, chronic kidney disease and stomach cancer.

Natural Disasters

Portugal is prone to a few natural disasters, namely forest fires, earthquakes, and floods.Earthquakes in Portugal don't occur frequently, but when they do they are often of high magnitude. The two earthquakes (November 1 1755 and February 28 1969) with greater magnitude in Europe since the year 1000 were generated off the coast of Portugal, and were felt intensely in Lisbon and Algarve.
